A diagram of the 5-step course design process. Click on the hotspots to learn more.

During the Design Plan, collaborators work together to identify the Course Goals and an Assessment Strategy. The project plan is laid out, the timeline is confirmed, and any other specific requirements for the course are identified. Then the Course Outline is created, fleshing out the topics, sub-topics, lesson objectives, instructional sequence, weekly activities and the relevant content and media types. Next, the Prototype phase, a singular lesson template is created which includes content, assessments, and represents the overall the layout design. During Production, content is developed and assessments are created in eLearning Management System (ELMS) and Canvas. Finally, during the Quality Review phase, the course is checked for quality standards, adherence to Penn State copyright requirements and compliance with ADA criterions.
